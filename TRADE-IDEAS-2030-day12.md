# SMP League — Trade Ideas by Team

About 27% of the season is played (roughly 12 games each), and the top 4 of 10 teams make the playoffs. Cambridge (10-2) is the clear class of the league behind a dominant Wembanyama-led frontcourt, with Stony Brook (8-4) the only other team meaningfully clear of the pack; everyone from Manhattan through Waltham is bunched at .500-ish and fighting over the final two seeds. At the bottom, Rochester and Ithaca sit at 4-8 — and Ithaca is the cautionary tale, a genuinely talented but old, expensive roster going nowhere.

## CAM — Cambridge Platypuses (10-2) · **Contend** · Need: a young two-way, three-point-shooting (3-and-D) wing
Cambridge are clear contenders — 10-2, the #1 seed, and a top-4 lock behind the league's best frontcourt (Wembanyama 77 and Duren 74 anchor elite rim protection and rebounding, with 23-year-old Boozer 69/78-pot the ascending fourth piece next to Maxey and Haliburton). Their one real hole is the wing: after the top six there's a cliff of one-dimensional, aging pieces — Bridges (61, declining), MPJ (59, a shooter who can't defend), Dyson Daniels (56, 30 3pt) — so they badly need a durable 3-and-D wing. Best fit is Ithaca (4-8, tanking): send Keegan Murray ($22M) plus Dyson Daniels' expiring and a 2034 first for Ryan Dunn (65, age 27, 3-and-D forward, 64 DIQ, $24M) — ITH gets a rotation vet and a pick, CAM gets exactly the two-way shooting it lacks. Alternative: flip Michael Porter Jr. ($16M) and young Kingston Flemings (58/67-pot) to Toronto (5-6, stockpiling young wings) for Johnny Furphy (68, 25, 64 DIQ 3-and-D) or Kon Knueppel (67, 24, 82 3pt). The big constraint is the pick cupboard: **CAM has already traded its 2030, 2031, and 2032 firsts — only 2033 and 2034 remain** — so deal from young roster pieces and expirings, not from picks. Sitting just $3M over the $300M soft cap, they're essentially cap-neutral and can absorb a little salary; use Bridges' $16M expiring as clean salary filler. Prioritize one impact wing over depth dumpster-diving, and don't chase a redundant big — the frontcourt is already the best in the league.

## STO — Stony Brook Stingrays (8-4) · **Contend** · Need: a true rim-protecting, rebounding starting center
Stony Brook is a clear contender (8-4, #2, top-4 lock) built on a deep, versatile wing/forward corps — Anthony Black (69, elite finisher), Scottie Barnes (68, 9.7 reb/5.8 ast do-it-all), Jaylen Wells (67, 50% from three), plus SGA, Gradey Dick and a promising young PG in Josh Adcock (58/75-pot, 8 apg). The glaring hole is the middle: there is no rotation center — Barnes fake-fives it at 6'8", Zion offers zero rim protection or spacing, and the kids (Moreau, Cenac) aren't ready. The leverage is enormous: STO has the league's lowest payroll ($235M, ~$65M under the soft cap and collecting the tax distribution) and owns all five of its own firsts (2030-2034). Best move is to cash a wing plus a pick for the anchor: send Jaylen Wells ($28M) and a 2031 first to Toronto — which is buried under five centers and starving for wing shooting — for Walker Kessler (69, 71 hgt/89 reb), keeping the core intact. If Toronto instead moves Kessler to a rival, the fallback is a package of Gradey Dick plus Jalen Williams and a 2033 first to Durham for younger rim-runner Kel'el Ware (26, 70 hgt/85 reb) or Hansen Yang for size and youth. With this much room and pick capital, lead with picks rather than gutting the wing rotation, and make defensive size the single priority — a switchable, shot-blocking 5 is the difference between a 2-seed and a title threat.

## MAN — Manhattan Elephants (6-6) · **Retool** · Need: a real starting center (rim protection, size, defensive rebounding)
Manhattan is 6-6 and 5th, one tiebreak outside the four-team cut, with a genuinely good young perimeter core (Reed Sheppard 76, Cade Cunningham, Isaiah Evans 89 3pt) but no true big — its only "center" is 34-year-old Myles Turner at 12 mpg, it blocks nothing, and it gets out-sized inside. The fix is a controllable young center. Best target is Durham's Kel'el Ware (61, age 26, rim-runner, $22M through 2033): package Christian Braun ($32M expiring) plus a 2032 first — DUR (6-6, deep at the 5) gets a plug-and-play scoring wing on an expiring and a pick, and the ~$10M in savings nudges MAN back under the $300M cap into the tax distribution. Alternative for more size: send Christian Bell ($18M) plus buried Ja Morant ($18M) and a 2033 first to Toronto for Alex Sarr (63, age 25) and Chet Holmgren (58, $14M), two young, long bigs TOR can spare from its center glut. MAN's leverage is real — it owns all five of its own firsts and carries a mountain of 2030 expirings (Cunningham, Evans, Murray-Boyles $34M, Braun $32M) for matching. Finance note: MAN sits exactly at the $300M cap, so trimming even a few million flips it from $0 to a ~$18M under-cap check — prefer deals that dip under. Don't chase another scorer; spend the picks and expirings on size and interior defense, and thin the crowded expiring-wing group (Braun, Bell) rather than stacking more guards.

## GOO — Gooning Gooners (6-6) · **Retool** · Need: a rim-protecting starting center (lead-guard playmaker a close second)
Gooning holds the #4 (final playoff) seed at 6-6, and 23-year-old AJ Dybantsa (78, 32.7 ppg) is a genuine cornerstone — so this is a win-now-leaning retool, not a tank — but the roster is soft in the middle and needs consolidation. The glaring hole is a rim-protecting center: Dereck Lively plays just 14 mpg and there's no interior anchor, with a lead ball-handler (best is 59-ovr Jamal Shead) the next gap. The double-edged lever: GOO carries the league's highest payroll at $347M, well over the soft cap and paying tax, so every deal must be a 2-for-1 that trims money — and it owns all five of its own firsts as sweeteners. Best target is Toronto's Walker Kessler (69, 10.8 reb, 2.0 blk): send buried Kyshawn George ($16M/6 mpg) plus Dereck Lively ($18M) and a 2031 first — that lands the anchor and sheds ~$6M, and TOR (5-6, deep frontcourt) gets salary relief and a pick to lean into its youth. (If Stony Brook lands Kessler first, Toronto can instead move Alex Sarr or Chet Holmgren in the same framework.) For the guard need, target a shot-creating, floor-spacing lead guard — Durham's Walter Clayton Jr. is the ideal fit but Toronto is likely to win that bidding, so the realistic alternative is Queens' Jalen Suggs (63, two-way, 68 3pt) for Cason Wallace ($16M) plus a 2034 first. Prioritize spacing and defense around a non-shooting star (Dybantsa is just 26% from three): every incoming piece should protect the rim or make open threes, and use the picks and buried salary to consolidate rather than adding a fourth good-not-great forward.

## DUR — Durham Destroyers (6-6) · **Retool** · Need: a 3-and-D wing / point-of-attack defender
Durham is 6-6 and 5th on tiebreak, a hair outside the top-4 but with a positive +2.3 net rating, so nudge the roster rather than blow it up. The core is fine — Shaedon Sharpe (71, 21 ppg), the Sengun passing hub (97 pss, but a non-spacer), Clayton Jr. as the lone plus shooter (83 3pt) — but the glaring hole is perimeter defense: minutes-weighted team DIQ is just ~52, with Edwards (46), Sharpe (53) and Sengun (61) all leaky and no true point-of-attack stopper. The best assets are financial: at $275M they sit under the $300M cap (collecting redistribution) with ~$25M of room to absorb salary, and nearly the whole roster expires in 2030. The contract to move is Anthony Edwards ($43M for a 63-ovr guard sliding at 28). Target 1: send Edwards plus a 2032 first to Rochester (4-8, tanking) for young 3-and-D wing Drake Powell (24, 59 3pt/61 DIQ, $8M) and Bennedict Mathurin's ($19M) matching salary — DUR sheds a bad deal for a cheap two-way wing on its timeline, ROC gets a marquee scorer and a pick to sell. Target 2: flip Tyrese Proctor ($18M expiring) plus a 2031 first to Waltham (4-6, sliding) for Tyler Herro (81 3pt expiring) to inject shooting on the wing. Prioritize DIQ and catch-and-shoot threes next to Sengun over more shot creation — spacing plus a stopper is what turns this from 5th into a top-4 seed. Use the expirings and cap room now, while they still have value.

## QUE — Queens Pigeons (5-6) · **Retool** · Need: a true lead/on-ball point guard
Queens is 5-6 and 6th, squarely on the bubble (top 4 make it) with a solid, versatile roster but no top-end star (best is Mobley at 66, well behind CAM's Wembanyama and GOO's Dybantsa), so neither win-now aggression nor a full tank fits. The biggest hole is a real lead guard: the roster is absurdly frontcourt-heavy (Mobley, Anunoby, Newell, Gibbons, Salaun, Banchero, J. Johnson, Steinbach) with only Jalen Suggs as a real ballhandler and wing Ed Johnson (9.6 apg) forced to run point. The fix is to convert that surplus into guard help — but Durham's Walter Clayton Jr. (the obvious target) is likely to land in Toronto, so lead elsewhere: package OG Anunoby ($30M expiring) plus filler to Manhattan for KJ Simpson (G, 27, 59/61, $24M expiring) — near salary-neutral, and guard-rich MAN happily upgrades to a two-way wing for its bubble run. If leaning younger, flip a redundant big (Tidjane Salaun, 24, or Banchero) to Stony Brook for buried PG prospect Josh Adcock (21, 58/75-pot) plus a pick — STO cashes a spare guard for a young rim big it needs. Watch the money: payroll is $295M, just $5M under the cap and collecting an ~$18M distribution, so keep every deal roughly flat or forfeit that check. You own a clean full set of your own 2030-2034 firsts — spend one or two on the backcourt, since the build is now around 21-year-old Jake Gibbons (63/77), not the aging wings. Sell high on the frontcourt logjam and prioritize on-ball creation and youth on the perimeter over adding yet another big.

## TOR — Toronto Jays (5-6) · **Retool** · Need: a true lead point guard / half-court creator
Toronto is 5-6 (7th) but just one game out of the 4-seed, with a young core and a loaded pick chest — the biggest hole is a real lead guard. Kon Knueppel (67, 82 3pt) is carrying playmaking at 7.2 apg from the wing and $1M Espoir Ndinga is a nice overachiever but not a starting initiator, so add a table-setter. Best swing: use the extra 2030 first TOR owns (acquired from CAM) plus the bloated Cody Williams deal ($42M, 12 ppg on 30% from three) to land Durham's Walter Clayton Jr. (63, 6.7 apg/83 3pt, expiring) and 3-and-D wing Tyrese Proctor — TOR fixes the point and sheds a bad long-term contract, while rebuilding DUR takes back an ovr-upgrade wing and a real first. Alternatively, dump Cody Williams to a cap-flush rebuilder like Waltham (which has a guard glut in Scoot, Giddey, Fears, Peterson) — send Franz Wagner's $15M expiring plus Dylan Harper (24, 65-pot) and a 2031 first for Scoot Henderson (65, lead guard) or Josh Giddey (64, 5.6 apg). Toronto is deep at center (Kessler, Sarr, Holmgren, Ngongba, Markovic) and wing (Knueppel/Furphy/Buzelis/Williams/Harper), so it can afford to trade a big to a needy rival — but don't overpay for another wing. Sitting at ~$286M, just under the cap, use Wagner's expiring as filler rather than taking back long money, and consolidate two of the redundant 68-ovr wings plus a first to move up in class rather than collecting more 65-ovr forwards.

## WAL — Waltham Bears (4-6) · **Retool** · Need: a young, rim-protecting center
Waltham's real asset is a genuine young core — Cooper Flagg (23, 68/75-pot), Jeremiah Fears (23, 67/74, 15.6 ppg on a 93 OIQ), and sharpshooter Darryn Peterson (23, 65 3pt) — but the roster is capsized by aging vets on expirings and has no rim protection: 22-year-old Jayden Quaintance (56) is the only true center, and not one big grades 60+ in defensive IQ. The single biggest move is to convert the surplus of guards/wings and the four 2030 expirings (Booker $24M, Tatum $24M, Cam Thomas $16M, Markkanen $15M ≈ $79M of filler) into a young 5. Cleanest partner is Queens (glut of bigs, one real guard): send Scoot Henderson (26, 65) plus Cam Thomas's $16M expiring and a 2032 first for young center Tidjane Salaun (24, 62/66-pot) — QUE finally gets a lead ball-handler, WAL lands a cheap developmental 5 to pair with Quaintance. A second look at Toronto — buried under five centers with zero quality guards — can pry Alex Sarr (25) or Chet Holmgren for a Scoot/Giddey-plus-Thomas package, giving WAL a younger, longer rim-protector than Kessler and TOR the backcourt it lacks. Finance lever: WAL sits $20M over the soft cap (inflated by $16M of dead money and paying the tax), so shipping a couple of expiring vets gets it back under and flips it into a tax recipient, and it owns all five of its own firsts. Don't overpay to make the play-in this year; use the aging vets and one future first as currency, prioritize a center and defense, and protect Flagg/Fears/Peterson/Quaintance as untouchables.

## ROC — Rochester Dragons (4-8) · **Retool** · Need: a real center (frontcourt youth behind 35-year-old Giannis)
At 4-8 (9th) Rochester is only ~2 games out of the race but has no star ceiling — top guy Amen Thompson is 68 while the contenders run 74-78 — so the play is to rebalance a badly forward-heavy roster (seven F/PF types) rather than pure-tank. The screaming need is at center: 35-year-old Giannis (64) is the only true 5, with no young big behind him. ROC is a tax recipient (payroll $288M, $12M under the cap, collecting ~$18M) loaded with fat expiring 2030 deals to weaponize (Jovic $37M, Ighodaro $37M, LaMelo $35M, Ben Sheppard $29M, Brown $22M) plus all five of its own firsts. Move 1: flip Nikola Jovic ($37M expiring) plus Ben Sheppard ($29M expiring) to frontcourt-glutted, guard-thin Queens for Evan Mobley (66, $27M expiring) — an instant center upgrade that drops ROC further under the cap while handing QUE matching money and a stretch forward. Move 2: sell LaMelo Ball ($35M expiring, 20 ppg) to guard-thin Durham for young Kel'el Ware (C, 26, 61, signed through 2033) plus PG Matthew Black — swapping a rental for a center on ROC's timeline (this is the fallback if Manhattan hasn't already pried Ware loose). Backup lever: dangle aging Jaylen Brown ($22M) and Austin Reaves ($13M) to cap-rich Stony Brook (only $235M payroll, buying for a run) for a young flier like 20-year-old center Karim Moore (51/69-pot) to add frontcourt youth. Don't chase the 8-seed — every deal should add a center or youth while shedding expiring forwards you'll lose for nothing, and hold those five firsts since you have no pick surplus.

## ITH — Ithaca Thunder (4-8) · **Tank** · Need: youth + rim protection (cash in aging vets around 24-year-old Tre Johnson)
Sell and tank. Ithaca is the league's oldest team (29.0 avg age up top), carries a $322M payroll over the soft cap (paying tax), and sits 4-8 in 10th with essentially no path to the top four — a "win-2027" roster stuck in 2030. The one real building block is Tre Johnson (24, 72/77-pot, 19/4.5/3.7 on 40% from three), with Tyler Smith (25, 66/69, 46% 3P) a keeper; everything else is expensive, aging, and redundant. The best asset is draft capital: ITH owns 7 firsts, owes none, and holds two extra CAM firsts (2031, 2032) — plus a pile of expiring 2030 money (Jokic $30M, JJJ $18M, Traore $16M, Taylor $18M) that makes salary-matching easy. Move 1: ship Nikola Jokic (35, $30M expiring) to Stony Brook — an 8-4 contender with ~$65M of room and a thin frontcourt — for young PF Donnie Freeman (24, 59/62) plus a first; ITH gets a young body and another pick while shedding its biggest expiring, STO gets an elite playmaking hub for a playoff push. Move 2: attach filler to move Domantas Sabonis ($20M) or Coby White ($12M) to Manhattan — thin on proven size behind its young forwards — for a 25-and-under wing like Bilal Coulibaly (63/66) and a pick, dumping aging money and opening minutes. Move 3: send Jaren Jackson Jr. ($18M expiring) plus a spare 2032 CAM first to Rochester for young forward Ron Holland II (25, 62/64), swapping a 30-year-old rental for a cost-controlled piece. Stop paying the tax to lose: convert every 30-plus vet into youth or picks before their value evaporates, and keep the pick hoard unless a sub-24 cornerstone is on offer next to Tre Johnson.
